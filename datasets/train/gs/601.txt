Coles substance at his death was exceeding great, hee had daily in his house an hundred men seruants and xl. maides hee maintained beside aboue two or three hundred people, spinners and carders, and a great many other house-holders. His wife neuer after married, and at her death shee bestowed a mightie summe of money toward the maintaining of the new builded Monastery. Her daughter was most richly married to a Gentleman of great worship, by whom she had many children. And some say, that the riuer whereinto Cole was cast, did euer since carrie the name of Cole being called The riuer of Cole, and the Towne of Colebrooke. How diuers of the Clothiers wiues went to the Church-ing of Suttons wife of Salisbury, and of their merri-ment. Chap. 12.
