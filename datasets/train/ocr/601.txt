Coles substance at his death was exceeding great, hee had daily in his house an hundred men seruants and xl. maides hee maintained beside aboue two or three hundred people, spinners and carders, and a great many other house-holders. His wife neuer after married, and at her death shee bestowed a mightie summe of money toward the maintaining of the new budded Monastery. Her daughter was most richly married to a Gentleman of great worship, by whom she had many chddren.
